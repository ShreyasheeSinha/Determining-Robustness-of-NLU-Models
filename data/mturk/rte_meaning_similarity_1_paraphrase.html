Instructions

Thank you for participating in this HIT! You will evaluate how closely one sentence matches the meaning of another sentence. The goal is to improve comprehension of languages by computers: your assistance is crucial to building better technologies behind services like Amazon Alexa, Apple Siri, or Google Translate.

You will be presented with a "reference" sentence and 3 other sentences.  On a scale of 0 to 100, we would like you to evaluate how closely a sentence matches the meaning of the reference. 

A sentence with a score of 100 means it has an "identical meaning" to the reference sentence (it may even be the original sentence itself!) A score of 0 means the meaning of the sentence is irrelevant or contradicting to the reference.

Rarely, the sentences may contain materials some readers find offensive. If this happens, please mark it via the provided checkbox. We believe all or almost all of the sentences do not require this option.

How do I measure meaning?

The following are example sentence pairs and suggested scores.

  • The following sentence pairs have nearly or exactly the same meaning, expressed with nearly identical or identical language.
  • ***We judge the quality of your work partly based on if sentences completely identical to the reference receive a perfect score, so please make sure to give them 100. 
    • the student was hugged by the teacher
    • the student was hugged by the teacher
    • ----------------------------------------------------------------------------
    • the student was hugged by the teacher
    • the teacher hugged the student

    Score: 100

    Opposite/Irrelevant Identical Meaning

 

  • In the following example, most of the meaning is shared, and most people would agree the new sentence means the same, or almost the same thing as the reference:
    • the student was hugged by the teacher
    • the student was embraced by the teacher
    • ----------------------------------------------------------------------------
    • the student was hugged by the teacher
    • the student was hugged by the instructor

    Score: 92

    • ----------------------------------------------------------------------------
    • the student was hugged by the teacher
    • the student was not hugged by the teacher

    Score: 0

    Opposite/Irrelevant Identical Meaning

 

One way to interpret "meaning the same thing" is whether the new sentence could plausibly be used to describe the same thing as the reference (Identical Meaning), and whether the new sentence could be used to describe something contradicting or unrelated to the reference (Opposite/Irrelevant). 

 

  • The following sentence pair is related in meaning, but not identical. Thus, the sentence gets a score between 0 and 100. Your judgement of the similarity between the sentence pair might be different from ours: this is OK! 
  • Please use your best judgement and aim for consistency across your HITs.
    • the student was hugged by the teacher
    • the person embraced the student​

    Score: 60

    Opposite/Irrelevant Identical Meaning

 

  • Another example of similar but not identical in meaning, where this new sentence seems to be less identical in meaning than in the previous example, and therefore is assigned a lower score. 
    • the student was hugged by the teacher
    • the person touched the student​

    Score: 45

  • The following is even less similar in meaning:
    • the student was hugged by the teacher

    Score: 20

    Opposite/Irrelevant Identical Meaning

     

  • It's possible that the sentence tries to convey similar meaning to the reference, but is worded awkwardly (albeit grammatical). We would like you to give it a lower score (lower than if it were worded properly, given the meaning wouldn't change) to reflect this:
    • the student broke the record
    • The schooler bettered the record​

    Score: 76

    Opposite/Irrelevant Identical Meaning

     

Occassionally, sentences might contain minor errors such as wrong casing (e.g. not capitalized when should be), or extra space between one word ("don't" versus "do n't"). We kindly ask you to ignore these mistakes and base your evaluation on the meaning. We consider these sentences grammatical.

  • the student was hugged by the teacher
  • I don't think it 's good. We got ta go now.​
  • They're greed, and they ca n't get enough.​
